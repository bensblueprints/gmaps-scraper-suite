# 5 Apollo Leads Alternatives You Should Try in 2026

*For sales teams and solo operators who are tired of paying $100+/month for leads that could be scraped for free.*

Apollo.io has dominated B2B lead generation for years. But at $49–$99/month per seat (more for team plans), it's a significant cost — especially when most of its data comes from public sources like Google Maps, LinkedIn, and business directories. A growing number of sales professionals are turning to desktop scraping tools that offer the same raw data at a fraction of the price.

Here are five Apollo alternatives worth knowing about.

---

## 1. AtomicScraper — Best for High-Volume Google Maps Scraping

**Website:** atomicscraper.com  
**Price:** $297/month or $997 one-time lifetime license

AtomicScraper is a Windows desktop app that pulls business listings directly from Google Maps using headless Chromium. You pick an industry (restaurants, contractors, lawyers, HVAC — it covers virtually everything), enter a city, and let it run. In a typical session, you can pull hundreds of leads in under an hour.

What makes it stand out is the enrichment layer: after scraping, it visits each business's website, extracts the contact email, and identifies the platform the site is built on (WordPress, Shopify, Wix, Squarespace, and 10+ others). That platform data is genuinely useful if you're selling web design or SaaS.

The lifetime license model is the real win. Pay $997 once and scrape forever. Compare that to Apollo's $99/month, which runs $1,188 per year for data that may or may not match your local market.

**Best for:** Solo operators, local marketing agencies, and anyone targeting local businesses.

---

## 2. Discovery1 (discoveryoneleads.com) — Best for Home Services & Contractor Leads

**Website:** discoveryoneleads.com  
**Price:** $297/month or $997 one-time lifetime license

Discovery1 is a purpose-built variant of the same scraping engine, focused exclusively on the home services vertical. Its industry list reads like a directory of every trade you'd want to pitch: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, landscaping, general contractors, pest control, window cleaning, pressure washing, handyman, pool service, garage door repair, appliance repair, and painting.

If you're running a marketing agency, selling leads to contractors, or operating a home warranty company, this is your tool. The industry filters are pre-built and the search queries are optimized for each trade — searching "HVAC" automatically expands to cover "air conditioning repair," "heating and cooling," and "AC installation" to maximize coverage.

The orange interface is hard to miss in the taskbar, and data exports as CSV ready to drop into any CRM.

**Best for:** Home services agencies, contractor marketplaces, and local SEO companies.

---

## 3. Leads.Baby (leads.baby) — Best All-Rounder

**Website:** leads.baby  
**Price:** $297/month or $997 one-time lifetime license

The name is playful but the capabilities are serious. Leads.Baby covers 20 industries — restaurants, retail, real estate, dentists, chiropractors, gyms, lawyers, accountants, auto repair, beauty salons, pet services, photography, and more. It's the broadest coverage in the lineup.

The hot pink UI is deliberately different from the corporate blue that dominates B2B tools, and it makes a point: lead generation doesn't have to feel like enterprise software. Leads still include name, phone, email, website, platform, and rating, with real-time display as the scraper runs.

Where Leads.Baby shines is variety. If your pitch changes week to week — law firms this week, gyms next week — you want a tool that covers everything out of the box.

**Best for:** Freelance lead gen providers, outreach agencies, and multi-vertical businesses.

---

## 4. Lead Scraper Pro — Best for Scaling Across Cities

**Website:** leadripper.com  
**Price:** $297/month or $997 one-time lifetime license

Lead Scraper Pro is the flagship product in the suite and includes the most advanced bulk-scraping capability: a job queue system that lets you queue up thousands of city/industry combinations and run them sequentially. The built-in US Cities database covers 29,880 cities, which means you can queue "plumbers in every city in Texas" and walk away.

The queue is SQLite-backed, which means it survives crashes and restarts. If your laptop dies mid-scrape, restart and it picks up exactly where it left off.

For teams, the node-locking system lets you add up to N machines under one license for $99 per additional machine. This is much cheaper than buying separate Apollo seats.

**Best for:** Power users, agencies running mass outreach campaigns, and anyone building lead databases at scale.

---

## 5. ProspectHunter — Best for General B2B Prospecting

**Price:** $297/month or $997 one-time lifetime license

ProspectHunter takes a purple-themed, all-industry approach similar to Leads.Baby, with an interface optimized for B2B prospecting workflows. It covers all major industries without the home-services-only constraint of Discovery1.

The tool's differentiation is in its clean, minimal interface — the left panel is industry selection and config, the right panel is a real-time leads table with searchable, sortable columns. If you've used Apollo and appreciated its table view, this will feel immediately familiar.

**Best for:** Sales reps doing their own prospecting, marketing agencies, and early-stage startups that can't justify enterprise Apollo pricing.

## The Key Trade-off: Apollo vs. Desktop Scrapers

Apollo gives you a curated, deduplicated database with verified emails. You pay a premium for curation and for the company that maintains it.

Desktop scrapers give you raw Google Maps data — which is frequently more up to date than any static database, because Maps is updated constantly by business owners and Google's own crawlers. You get more local granularity, especially for small businesses that never show up in enterprise databases.

The sweet spot: use a desktop scraper to build your initial lead list, use Hunter.io or a mail validator to verify emails at scale, and run your outreach through Instantly or Smartlead. Total cost is still likely under what you'd pay for a single Apollo seat annually.
